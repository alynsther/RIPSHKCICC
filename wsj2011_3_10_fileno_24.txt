Reliance Communications Ltd. said Wednesday it has signed final papers for a loan of $1.93 billion from Chinese banks and financial institutions.

The loan will be used to refinance debt taken previously to pay bandwidth fees and to buy equipment, India's second-largest mobile phone operator by users said in a statement. The loan matures in 10 years and will help save interest costs, it said.

China Development Bank has fully underwritten the loan and is also part of the syndicate providing the debt, Reliance Communications said.

Of the loan, $600 million will be used to finance the purchase of Chinese telecom equipment from Huawei Technologies Co. and  ZTE Corp.
