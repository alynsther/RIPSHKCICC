Negative cues from Asian and European markets over concerns about the euro zone's debt woes following the arrest of International Monetary Fund chief Dominique Strauss-Kahn further soured investor sentiment. 

The Bombay Stock Exchange's Sensitive Index fell 186.25 points, or 1.0%, to close at 18345.03 after moving between 18319.88 and 18492.68. On the National Stock Exchange, the 50-stock S&P CNX Nifty fell 45.75 points, or 0.8%, to end at 5499.00. 

Trading volume on the BSE fell sharply to 21.03 billion rupees ($468.9 million) from Friday's 28.33 billion rupees. Gainers lagged decliners 1,052 to 1,713, while 134 stocks were unchanged. 

In Asia, Japan's Nikkei fell 0.9% to 9558.30, Hong Kong's Hang Seng index shed 1.4% to 22960.63 and China's Shanghai Composite declined 0.8% to 2849.07. In European markets, France's CAC and Germany's DAX were both trading down 1.4%. 

Markets in India opened weak after the three state-owned fuel retailers raised gasoline prices by five rupees per liter over the weekend. Expectations are that the government may soon let oil retailers increase diesel and cooking fuel prices as well, which might stoke inflation and could prompt the central bank to further tighten monetary policy. 

Government data released Monday showed inflation based on the wholesale price index rose 8.66% in April from a year earlier, slower than a provisional 9.04% increase in March, but above the 8.50% average estimate in a poll of 15 economists. 

"The fuel price hike and negative global cues weighed on the [Indian] market and the inflation reading was also above estimates," said Ambareesh Baliga, chief operating officer at Way2Wealth Securities. 

Analysts expect the Sensex to be range-bound in coming sessions until there is clarity on diesel and cooking fuel prices. 

Of the 30 Sensex constituents, 26 ended lower Monday, with losses led by interest rate-sensitive stocks due to inflation concerns. 

ICICI Bank, the country's largest lender by assets, fell 1.7% to 1,057.45 rupees and mortgage lender Housing Development Finance Corp. shed 2.0% to 633.75 rupees.

Autos fell due to concerns that the increase in gasoline prices will further reduce demand for cars. Mahindra & Mahindra slipped 2.7% to 673.15 rupees and Bajaj Auto closed 2.7% lower at 1,300.85 rupees.

Losses in real estate were led by DLF, which closed down 2.6% at 226.20 rupees.

Jaiprakash Associates fell 3.2% to 84.75 rupees after posting disappointing January-March results over the weekend. 

Among the handful of gainers, Hero Honda Motors rose 3.9% to 1,864.95 rupees on bargain buying and on expectations the company will soon announce a record date for its 2010-11 dividend pay-out.
