Media reports Tuesday said the Congress party, which leads the federal coalition government, has resumed talks with Dravida Munnettra Kazhagam, a regional party from South India after it threatened to pull out of the ruling coalition. 

The Bombay Stock Exchange's Sensitive Index rose 216.98 points, or 1.2%, to close at 18439.65. The trading volume on the BSE rose to 28.68 billion rupees ($637.2 million) from Monday's 24.33 billion rupees. 

Gainers outnumbered decliners 1,778 to 1,059, while 124 stocks were unchanged. 

On the National Stock Exchange, the 50-stock S&P CNX Nifty rose 57.65 points, or 1.1%, to end at 5520.8. 

"The market managed to hold gains as domestic political concerns eased, but concerns over the situation in MENA [the Middle East and North Africa region] and high oil prices persist," said  Arun Kejriwal, director at KRIS Capital. 

April Nymex crude oil futures, which surged to a fresh 29-month high of $106.95 a barrel on Monday, took a breather and the contract was last trading down 108 cents at $104.36 on Globex. 

Of the 30 Sensex constituents, 25 ended higher Tuesday. 

Software giant Infosys Technologies, which has a 10.2% weight in the Sensex, rose 2.5% to 3,114.65 rupees on expectations that outsourcing demand from overseas clients will remain robust. 

Banking saw a round of bargain buying following Monday's losses. ICICI Bank, India's second-largest lender by assets, gained 1.3% to 1,020.85 rupees while  HDFC Bank climbed 1.3% to 2,206.05 rupees. 

Tata Steel led gains in metals after the company said it has entered into a tie-up with a Canadian mining firm to develop potentially large-scale iron-ore reserves in Labrador and Quebec in Canada. The stock rose 1.9% to 614.55 rupees while the metal sub-index gained 1.5%. 

Among other Sensex gainers, Bharti Airtel closed 3.2% higher at 334.2 rupees while  Tata Motors gained 2.9% to 1,160.05 rupees. 

Tech Mahindra rose 9.3% to 739.35 rupees while Satyam Computer Services finished 3.8% higher at 64.9 rupees on hopes of indirectly benefiting from Mahindra Group flagship firm Mahindra & Mahindra's global pact with  Cisco Systems.
