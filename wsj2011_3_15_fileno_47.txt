The Bombay Stock Exchange's Sensitive Index fell 271.84 points, or 1.5%, to close at 18,167.64. 

On the National Stock Exchange, the 50-stock S&P CNX Nifty fell 81.85 points, or 1.5%, to end at 5,449.65. 

  Will the Quake Shake Indian Markets?  

Trading volume on the BSE rose to 36.45 billion rupees ($810 million) from Monday's 29.75 billion rupees. Gainers lagged decliners 847 to 1,979, while 101 stocks were unchanged. 

Among Asian markets, Japan's Nikkei plunged 10.6%, extending Monday's 6.2% fall, pressured by news of more explosions at Tokyo Electric Power Co.'s Fukushima Daiichi nuclear power plant. Hong Kong's Hang Seng fell 2.9% while China's Shanghai Composite closed 1.4% lower. 

In Europe, France's CAC was trading 5.0% lower while Germany's DAX was down 3.9%. 

"The losses in the Sensex would have been much steeper if not for Reliance. Markets across the globe are fearful of the collateral damage from the situation in Japan," said Deven Choksey, managing director at K.R. Choksey Shares and Securities. 

Analysts expect news from the unfolding nuclear crisis in Japan to decide the direction of the Indian market in the coming sessions. 

All but two of the Sensex's 30 constituents ended in the red Tuesday. 

The benchmark index was supported by gains in Reliance Industries, India's largest company by market capitalization. The company's shares rose 1.8% to 1,036.3 rupees on expectations the global demand for refined fuel will increase following supply disruptions at Japanese refineries. 

Reliance Communications ended up 0.5% at 100.95 rupees.

Infosys Technologies, which has a more than 10% weight in the Sensex, fell 2.2% to 3,029.7 rupees, while India's second-largest lender ICICI Bank slipped 2.2% to 996.45 rupees. 

Car maker Maruti Suzuki, which sources 25% of its auto parts from Japan, led the losses in autos. It shed 3.6% to 1,214.5 rupees while Mahindra & Mahindra closed 2.7% lower at 644.35 rupees. 

Real estate was the biggest sector to lose with the realty sub-index closing down 3.1%. 

The Bombay Stock Exchange's benchmark Sensex was trading down 2.3% at 18,012.73 with all 30 constituent stocks trading in the red.

Japan's Nikkei plunged 12.8% following news of explosions at the Fukushima Daiichi nuclear power plant Tuesday, this time at its No. 2 and No. 4 reactors. Hong Kong's Hang Seng was trading 3.9% lower while China's Shanghai Composite was down 2.1%.

Sensex losses are led by Reliance Industries, which is down 2.2% at 995.10 rupees, while  Infosys Technologies is 2.4% lower at 3,025.00 rupees.

Among the BSE's sectoral indices, the metal index is down 2.8% while the auto index is trading 2.7% lower.
