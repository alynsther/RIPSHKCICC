Stocks gained ground in both Asia and Europe as strength in commodity prices boosted miners and investors reacted to the Federal Reserve's unanimous decision to keep buying bonds to drive down borrowing costs. 

China's benchmark stock index rose 1.5% despite continued government efforts to control home prices. The Shanghai Composite advanced 1.5% to 2749.15. Property developers tumbled after Beijing decided to raise the minimum down payment on second-home purchases to 60% from 50% Wednesday. 

In Europe, the Stoxx Europe 600 index advanced 0.2% to 282.88. Losses for bank stocks had initially pulled the index lower after Standard & Poor's cut Japan's long-term sovereign-debt rating by one notch to AA minus from AA. But the sector swiftly recovered as the focus swung back to the Fed decision. 

"Japanese debt is held primarily by the Japanese, so it's not clear to me why everyone is so worried," said  Peter Dixon, a strategist at Commerzbank. If Japan were to default, it would be defaulting on debts owed to the Japanese people, rather than foreign banks, he noted. 

"What we're more focused on right now is earnings season, which so far is going well," Mr. Dixon added.
