Mumbai--Capital market regulator Securities and Exchange Board of India gave India Infoline Ltd its final nod to start its mutual fund business.

The financial services firm plans to raise money from foreign institutions for a fund of funds. 

  Umang Papneja, president (products) of India Infoline, had said earlier that "the fund will invest in a diversified portfolio of Indian mutual funds across eight to 10 asset managers. The investments could be in equity-related mutual funds and would be diversified by industry, sector and managers."

In its filing with the Bombay Stock Exchange, the company said the approval will allow it to start its mutual fund business, and launch plans over time.
