Sales of exiting homes rose 12.3% in December to an annual rate of 5.28 million units, beating economists' forecasts. Still, only 4.9 million homes were sold in 2010, the fewest since 1997—though some see last month's increase as a sign that the housing market has bottomed.

The SEC said it needed more money to oversee investment advisers, and gave Congress three options: Impose a fee on the industry to cover its costs, set up a self-regulating organization or let brokerage regulator Finra do the job.

The number of Americans filing for initial jobless benefits fell to 404,000 last week, the Labor Department said—the fewest since the week ended Dec. 25 and a drop from the prior week's 441,000.
