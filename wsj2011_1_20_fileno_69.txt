While he has his detractors, many say that Damien Hirst is a genius. In certain circles, he and his artwork — the shark in a tank full of formaldehyde, for instance — epitomize contemporary art.

"I want to blow people away, I want people to gasp," Mr. Hirst said while in Hong Kong recently for a small solo show of his work at the opening of the first Gagosian gallery in Asia. Indeed, the diamond-covered baby skull that's on display in Hong Kong — a version is now on exhibit in Florence — is shocking to some, and considered hardly art by others.
