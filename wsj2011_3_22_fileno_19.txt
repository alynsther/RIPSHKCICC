 The first name of U.S. Energy Department Secretary Steven Chu was misspelled as Stephen in a Disaster in Japan article on Monday about tests at U.S. nuclear-energy facilities.

 The Dow Jones Industrial Average declined 1.5% last week. A contents item on the front of Saturday's Business & Finance section incorrectly said that it had risen by that amount.

 Japanese regulators limit radioactivity in milk to 300 becquerels per kilogram of raw milk and 2,000 becquerels per kilogram for spinach. A Disaster in Japan article about radiation in food in some editions Monday incorrectly said in one instance that Japanese regulators set the limit for milk at 2,000 becquerels per kilogram.
