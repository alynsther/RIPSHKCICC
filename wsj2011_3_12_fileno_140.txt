Sports car maker Ferrari said Thursday it has signed an agreement with India's Shreyans group to sell four models in the country by spring this year.

Shreyans group will import and sell Ferrari cars through two dealerships, the first of which will open in New Delhi by spring, the  Fiat S.p.A. (F.MI) unit said in a statement.

The second Ferrari dealership at Mumbai will be operational in the second half of this year, it said.

Ferrari plans to sell its California, 458 Italia, 599 GTB Fiorani and FF car models in India.

Shreyans group also will set up an independent office for Ferrari in India, the statement added.
