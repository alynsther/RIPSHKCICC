Japan’s Current Account Soars to Highest Surplus in Seven Years

By
Eleanor Warnock
  • Eleanor Warnock
    The Wall Street Journal
    CANCEL
  • BiographyEleanor Warnock
  • @misssaxbys
  • Google+
May 12, 2015 8:47 p.m. ET

TOKYO—Japan posted its biggest current account surplus in seven years in March, as a jump in overseas investment income and a fall in imports put the balance back at a level last seen regularly before the global financial crisis.

The surplus in the current account, the broadest measure of Japan’s trade with the rest of the world, was ¥2.80 trillion ($23.3 billion) in March before seasonal adjustment, government data showed...
