The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 0.3% this week, its second consecutive drop, as weaker data increased concern that the U.S. economy is headed for a slowdown. The S&P 500 dropped 0.2%, while the Nasdaq Composite finished the week up 0.03%.

Prices in the U.S. rose across the board in April, the Bureau of Labor Statistics said, though some economists say inflation pressures may be receding. The U.S. core consumer price index rose 0.2% in April, up from 0.1% in March and in line with economic forecasts. 

The average 401(k) balance rose to $74,900 at the end of March, Fidelity said, the highest since the firm began tracking the data in 1998 and a 12% jump from a year ago. Nearly 10% of participants raised their contribution rate during the first quarter, also a record.
