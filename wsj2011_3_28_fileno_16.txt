Mr. Pramanik, previously managing director of  Oracle Corp.'s India arm, replaces  Ravi Venkatesan, who said in February he planned to step down after a seven-year stint at the U.S. company. 

As chairman, Mr. Pramanik will oversee Microsoft's sales, marketing and services unit and head Microsoft's assets in India, the U.S. company said in a statement. 

Mr. Pramanik begins his assignment with Microsoft Monday, it added. 

Mr. Pramanik will report to  Jean-Philippe Courtois, president of Microsoft International. 

Microsoft has more than 5,300 employees, housed in two campuses and 13 offices, across India, making it the largest employee base outside of the U.S. for the company.
