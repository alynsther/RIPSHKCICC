NEW DELHI -- SpiceJet Ltd. Thursday posted a 13% decline in its third-quarter net profit at 944.5 million rupees ($20.8 million) compared with 1.09 billion rupees a year earlier.

Sales in the three months ended Dec. 31 rose 27% to 8.20 billion rupees from 6.47 billion rupees, India's second-biggest budget carrier by market share said.

SpiceJet said October-December was its fifth straight quarter of profit, when the airline also posted its highest-ever quarterly passenger load factor of 87.8%.

The carrier had a load factor of 80% a year earlier.
