The Dow Jones Industrial Average briefly traded above 12000 twice this week but finished down 0.4% on fears the Egyptian government would collapse. Disappointing earnings reports from companies also contributed to market weakness, while oil rose 4.3% on Friday alone.

Home prices fell 1% in November, the fourth month in a row of declines, according to the Case-Shiller index. But sales are picking up: New-home purchases rose 17.5% in December, the largest month-over-month jump since 1992, the Commerce Department said. 

A new proposal from the Securities and Exchange Commission would exclude the full value of homes from one's net worth to determine who is an "accredited investor." That means many people now able to invest in hedge funds may no longer be able to do so.
